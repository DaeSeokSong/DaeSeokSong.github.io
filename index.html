About Me & Research Objective

As an AI Engineer with three years of experience tackling real-world efficiency challenges in the medical AI industry, I have developed a strong conviction. My work, which involved optimizing AI systems for CPU-based on-premise environments under strict hospital security policies, revealed the fundamental physical limitations (power, space) of the current von Neumann architecture.

This realization has guided my focus toward **Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) and Neuromorphic Computing** as the solution. My core research objective is to make this efficient, 3rd-generation neural network universally accessible. Ultimately, I aim to establish a **general-purpose learning methodology for SNNs**, much like Geoffrey Hinton did for ANNs with Backpropagation, to contribute to the future of sustainable AI.
